Mitigating The Risk of a Data Breach When Recycling Your Data Center Assets

Data Centers pose unique risks, making it even more of priority to find an ITAD partner specializing in Data Center Projects .
There are different Data Center procedures and protocols to avoid adverse security events, and those protocols go beyond managing hard drive information.
Make sure you have a broader perspective of upgrading to new devices and equipment and replacing the old entails. A list of decommissioned includes the following examples:

  • Wireless routers
  • Switches
  • Laptops and Desktop Computers
  • Hard drives
  • External drives
  • disk drives
  • Memory
  • Servers

Eliminating Data At Rest in Your Data Center Decommissioning Project

One of the principal vulnerabilities that may end up in your devices is Data At Rest.
As part of mitigating the risk of a data breach, Data at Rest needs to be eliminated from any storage and media components in your devices. This is something that is often missed by many other ITAD and Data Center Recycling Providers.
Brass Valley will ensure that the information, including data at rest, is eliminated from any computers, servers, and other data storing devices.
Responsible companies do a great job protecting the data while they are inside the data center. Let’s ensure the data is eliminated and destroyed before it leaves your data center as your assets get recycled.

Data Center Recycling Methods that Meet Modern Day Environmental Safety Standards

You will also want to know that your methods of electronic disposal meet modern-day environmental safety standards.
Brass Valley has multiple ITAD and ISO Certifications to ensure your equipment is processed to the most exacting standards in the industry.

When Compliance is Top Priority for Cyber Security and Environmental Standards

Every organization is responsible for ensuring that Cybersecurity and Environmental Safety Standards compliance is a top priority.
Protecting data is critical to limiting its exposure to external threats. Protecting the Environment and following Environmental Safety Standards is a must for any organization.

The world has reached a critical stage with viruses, and hackers and organizations are starting to understand the vital need to protect themselves. It becomes a top priority to remain compliant and improve their compliance standards in each step they take.

These organizations also want to make sure that they are compliant with local Environmental Laws as with newer Global trends in that process.
Time is ticking, and organizations must address these issues across their organizations. Datacenter decommissioning is an excellent step in that direction.
